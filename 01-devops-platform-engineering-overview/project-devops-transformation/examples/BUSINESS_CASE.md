# Business Case — Apex DevOps Transformation (Reference)

## Problem statement

Apex Logistics loses revenue and margin to slow releases, long incidents, and manual compliance work. Continuing current ops growth requires **4 additional hires ($600K/year)** with no guarantee of DORA improvement.

## Proposed investment

| Item | Year 1 cost |
|------|-------------|
| Platform team (existing 4 FTE re-focused) | $0 incremental headcount |
| Tooling (CI minutes, IaC, security) | $120K |
| Training & enablement | $80K |
| Contractor buffer (audit sprint) | $150K |
| **Total program** | **$850K** (board approved) |

## Expected benefits (Year 1)

| Benefit | Conservative | Basis |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Avoided ops hires | $600K | Cap headcount per CFO |
| SLA credit reduction | $120K | MTTR 6h → 1h |
| Contractor onboarding reduction | $100K | 10-day → 3-day ramp |
| Revenue retention | $400K | Prevent repeat lost-deal pattern |
| **Total benefit** | **$1.22M** | |

## ROI

- **Net benefit Year 1:** $1.22M − $850K = **$370K**
- **ROI:** 44% first year; higher in Year 2 when tooling costs drop
- **Payback:** ~8 months on SLA + onboarding savings alone

## Non-financial wins

- SOC2 observation passed
- Sales credible on weekly API releases
- Engineer satisfaction + retention

## Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Squad resistance | Pilot on playback-api; show metrics |
| Audit deadline slip | Phase 1 priority; executive sponsor |
| Tool sprawl | TaskFlow single roadmap |

## Recommendation

Approve Phase 1 immediately; tie executive bonuses to DORA + SOC2 milestones tracked in TaskFlow.
